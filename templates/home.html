Solitons Capstone Project

Solitons and Numerical Methods

Solitons are unique wave phenomena that do not lose energy over time due to their non-linear nature, characterized by the Korteweg–De Vries equation and Nonlinear Schrödinger equations.

In this capstone project, various numerical methods were employed to solve for soliton solutions. The split-step Fourier method for the KDV equation faced overflow issues, and the finite difference method via NLSE was stable but required adjustments for proper functioning, leading to a static animation.

Two-Wave Page

Explores the behavior of two solitons potentially superposing on one another. If wave 2 is faster than wave 1, the effect will be demonstrated on screen. An option to examine all 3 waves on the same graph is also provided for better understanding of superposition.

Shallow Water Wave Page

Illustrates the special dispersive effects of solitons, particularly in cases of varying water depth. Apply a sin function to the water depth and add a dispersion term to observe interesting cases with potential applications in simulating water waves in rivers or coastal regions.
